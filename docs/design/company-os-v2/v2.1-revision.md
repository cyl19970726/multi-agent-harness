## Why this revision exists

V2.0 established the correct product hierarchy but still contains too much
administrative chrome, equal-weight bordered containers, and generated
navigation inconsistency. V2.1 concentrates on the three pages that define
daily experience and brand identity before expanding the remaining page family.

V2.1 does not move away from the workbench model. It aims for a desirable
workbench: capable and information-rich, but comfortable, beautiful, and
inviting enough that visual quality itself improves concentration and use.

## Shared V2.1 constraints

- canonical navigation is exactly `Home / Docs / Organization / Work /
  Approvals / Finance`, plus Execution `Missions / Workflows / Agent Teams`;
- reduce visible borders and cards by roughly one third;
- one dominant first-viewport focal area per page;
- use typography, whitespace, subtle material changes, and alignment before
  bordered containers;
- integrate stable role-specific Agent avatars without inferring authority;
- use concise fixture-aligned copy and July 2026 dates where dates are visible;
- no Project, Task Graph, raw provider log, persisted thinking, copied finance
  facts, or unsupported active controls.

## Page revisions

### Home

Move from a dashboard composition to an editorial daily brief. Human decision
is the only high-contrast surface. The Company Lead brief becomes a recognisable
Agent-authored narrative with its stable avatar. Milestones read as a calm
operating sequence, not three equal cards.

### Docs

Use one global product rail and one compact page tree, but make the document
paper the dominant surface. Embedded live views should feel like part of the
document. Comments and Agent maintenance remain subtle edge context. The right
rail is light and collapsible in the eventual implementation.

### Lead Agent

Remove message-card stacking. Human and Agent conversation flows naturally in
one central stream; WorkItem, Milestone, Mission summary, evidence, and proposal
appear as embedded objects. Direct-report avatars create scan identity. Durable
Activity and transient live presence remain visually distinct.
