# Company OS V2.2 — Art-directed Workbench

## Decision

Keep the V2 product layout and functionality. Add a deliberate visual asset
layer so the interface feels recognisable, comfortable, and complete without
becoming a marketing surface.

## What stays fixed

- the six P0 mother pages and their product questions;
- the shared shell, page-specific composition, and context-rail semantics;
- Human, Standing Agent, External, and temporary execution identity boundaries;
- Milestone → WorkItem as the only Work hierarchy;
- Mission, Wave, Agent Team, and Workflow as one-time execution drill-ins;
- pending Approval, Commitment, and Payment remain visibly distinct.

## What changes

- canonical Agent portraits become a first-class identity layer;
- one vector icon family covers product objects and WorkItem types;
- major Business Modules gain role-specific emblems and cover art;
- page headers gain one restrained visual anchor where it improves orientation;
- selected pressure states receive richer but still semantic material treatment;
- subtle texture and micro-motion make long work sessions feel less sterile.

## Page treatment

| Page | Dominant art anchor | Supporting treatment | Must remain quiet |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Home | Lead briefing portrait/orbit | editorial date mark and company pulse line | Milestone rows |
| Docs | knowledge constellation / document architecture motif | module emblems in the tree | document body and relation rail |
| Organization | individual canonical portraits and role halos | reporting-line flow and proposal seal | authority copy |
| Lead Agent | Lead portrait and subtle coordination field | direct-report portrait strip and live presence | durable activity stream |
| Business Module | Trademark emblem and filing-lifecycle cover motif | jurisdiction/evidence marks | WorkItem and finance truth tables |
| Work | Milestone route marker | typed WorkItem glyphs and responsibility avatars | ledger rows and filters |

## Review and implementation gate

The Human Owner approved V2.2 as the implementation direction by explicitly
starting the implementation round. The generated pages remain design evidence,
not runtime truth: production code must source labels, dates, relationships,
status, and authority from its projection.

All six mother pages now have deterministic-fixture browser renders in
[`actual/`](actual/) and one triptych review gallery in
[`expected-vs-actual-v2.2.html`](expected-vs-actual-v2.2.html). Their status is
`implementation_review_pending` until the Human Owner reviews the Actual
renders. This does not claim Store-live persistence or approve any company
action represented by the fixture.
